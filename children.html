Child #1: Shawn Robinson

Shawn Robinson grew up in the mean streets of Bankston, Ohio. Contemporary readers may only know Bankston for its suburbanite shopping malls and drive thru movie theaters, and thus not remember the history of the place. This was a different Bankston altogether, a freshly made Hooverville -- with an even fresher bitterness harbored in the hearts of all the young families it housed.

These families held tightly onto an anger that only a broken promise can produce. They had been assured of a chance at an American Dream that seemed to now be wisping away with every dusty cloud of wind that pelted their shacks.

And it was into this very cloud of animosity, betrayal, and frustration, that young Shawn Robinson first played the piano. It belonged to their next door neighbor, Wally. Shawn was not invited over. Nor was he welcome.

His first piece, entirely from intuiton, was Fur Elise, but actually even better than how Beethoven could play it. That's what everyone always said. "This is just like Beethoven's Fur Elise, but better because of all those extra little things Shawn is doing right now. Like, the notes and the way he's holding them, that makes it better right now."

With a wink and a soft smile, might I just allude that the rest, dear reader, has been a well documented and scandalous ride that I'm sure you're already quite aware of. Adieu.
